What is a Bubble tea?

Bubble tea (also known as pearl milk tea, bubble milk tea, or boba) is a tea-based drink. Originating in Taichung, Taiwan in the early 1980s, it includes chewy tapioca balls ("boba" or "pearls") or a wide range of other toppings.

Ice-blended versions are frozen and put into a blender, resulting in a slushy consistency. There are many varieties of the drink with a wide range of flavors. The two most popular varieties are black pearl milk tea and green pearl milk tea.

A constantly expanding market

Throughout the past decade, a large number of bubble tea companies have been making their way in most Western cities, also shifting to higher quality drinks. What was once rare and hard to come by, has finally become a drink one can easily find and enjoy.

Make your own bubble tea at home

Some essential ingredients you will need for a simple (yet perfect) milk tea:

  • Good quality loose-leaf black tea
  • Tapioca pearls (starch from cassava roots)
  • Milk of your choice
  • Sweetener (honey, syrup)
